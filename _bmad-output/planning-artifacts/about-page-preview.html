My Story

For over a decade, I've been fortunate enough to witness and capture some of nature's most extraordinary moments. From the vast savannas of the Serengeti to the dense tropical forests of Costa Rica, my journey as a wildlife photographer has been driven by a deep passion for conservation and a commitment to sharing the untamed beauty of our natural world.

My approach to wildlife photography is rooted in patience, respect, and ethical practices. I believe that great wildlife photography comes not from disturbing or manipulating nature, but from understanding animal behavior, reading the environment, and being present in the moment. Every photograph tells a story—not just of the animal itself, but of the ecosystem it inhabits and the urgent need to protect these wild spaces.

Through my work, I aim to inspire others to appreciate and protect the incredible biodiversity of our planet. Whether photographing elephants in Amboseli, tigers in Ranthambore, or scarlet macaws in the Amazon, my goal remains the same: to create images that connect people emotionally with wildlife and motivate action toward conservation.

Equipment & Approach

Great wildlife photography requires more than just good gear—it demands knowledge, patience, and an ethical approach to working with wildlife. That said, having reliable equipment is essential for capturing fleeting moments in challenging conditions.

Primary Camera Bodies

  • Canon EOS R5 - for versatility and high-resolution captures
  • Nikon Z9 - for fast action and low-light performance
  • Sony A1 - for incredible autofocus and continuous shooting

Lenses

  • Canon RF 100-500mm f/4.5-7.1L IS USM - telephoto zoom for wildlife
  • Nikon Z 400mm f/2.8 TC VR S - prime telephoto for fast action
  • Sony FE 200-600mm f/5.6-6.3 G OSS - versatile reach and sharpness
  • Canon RF 100mm f/2.8L Macro IS USM - for intimate close-ups

Field Approach

  • Patience and silent observation to understand animal behavior
  • Ethical wildlife practices—never baiting, disturbing, or manipulating subjects
  • Understanding ecosystems and habitats to anticipate wildlife movements
  • Working with local guides and conservationists for responsible access
  • Minimal environmental impact—leave no trace philosophy

Conservation & Mission

My mission is simple: to use photography as a powerful tool for wildlife conservation and environmental education. Every image I capture is not just about aesthetics—it's about raising awareness, inspiring protection, and supporting the preservation of endangered species and threatened ecosystems.

I am committed to ethical wildlife photography practices that prioritize animal welfare and habitat preservation over the perfect shot. This means respecting wildlife boundaries, never interfering with natural behaviors, and supporting conservation organizations working on the ground to protect these species and their habitats.

Through partnerships with conservation groups, educational programs, and advocacy efforts, I strive to contribute meaningfully to the global movement to protect our planet's incredible biodiversity. I believe that by sharing the beauty and vulnerability of wildlife, we can inspire the next generation of conservationists and create a future where humans and nature coexist in harmony.
