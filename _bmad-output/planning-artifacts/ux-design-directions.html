Design Direction Variations

Each direction explores different ways to present your wildlife photography while maintaining our matte nature palette, calm aesthetic, and photography-first principles. Consider which approach best supports your goal of becoming the go-to wildlife portfolio.

Direction 1: Gallery First

Immediate Impact High Density Browse-Forward

Homepage IS the gallery. Featured image at top + immediate masonry grid below. Minimal navigation overlay. Photography dominates from first pixel.

Strengths:

  • Maximum photography visibility
  • Immediate showcase of portfolio breadth
  • Familiar Pinterest-like pattern
  • Fast time-to-engagement

Trade-offs:

  • Less focus on single hero moment
  • May overwhelm with too many choices
  • Harder to establish narrative/story

Direction 2: Hero Dominant

Full-Screen Impact Progressive Disclosure Cinematic

Full-screen time-adaptive hero fills 100vh. Navigation/CTA overlays appear after 3 seconds. Scroll reveals gallery. Maximum first impression impact.

Strengths:

  • Instant "wow" moment with single image
  • Perfect for time-adaptive hero feature
  • Clear visual hierarchy and storytelling
  • Supports progressive disclosure pattern

Trade-offs:

  • Requires scroll to see more work
  • Success depends heavily on hero image quality
  • Less portfolio breadth shown immediately

Behind-the-lens story appears prominently alongside featured image. Educational and engaging narrative.

Direction 3: Storyteller

Content-Rich Educational Balanced Layout

Featured image + prominent narrative content (behind-the-lens stories, camera settings, location context) displayed side-by-side. Emphasizes educational value.

Strengths:

  • Appeals to photography enthusiasts
  • Showcases expertise through storytelling
  • Balanced visual + text content
  • Differentiates from pure gallery portfolios

Trade-offs:

  • More text could distract from images
  • Requires content creation for each feature
  • Less pure "photography dominates" aesthetic

Direction 4: Masonry Homepage

Grid-Forward No Single Hero Democratic Showcase

Skip single hero entirely. Homepage opens directly to masonry grid of best work (20-30 images). Minimal header with photographer name. Work speaks immediately.

Strengths:

  • Showcases portfolio breadth instantly
  • All images get equal prominence
  • Ultra-minimal UI approach
  • Fast path to browsing full collection

Trade-offs:

  • No single "hero moment" for impact
  • Can't leverage time-adaptive hero feature
  • May feel overwhelming/busy immediately

Direction 5: Minimal Navigator

Typography-Led Elegant Restraint Choice-Forward

Centered typography with subtle time-adaptive image background (low opacity/blur). Clear navigation choices presented upfront. Elegant, unhurried introduction.

Strengths:

  • Maximum elegance and sophistication
  • Clear user choice (Color vs B&W)
  • Can still use time-adaptive background
  • Calm, unhurried first impression

Trade-offs:

  • Less immediate photography impact
  • Requires user decision before seeing work
  • May feel too minimal/sparse

Direction 6: Split Experience

Asymmetric Context-Rich Desktop-Optimized

Desktop: Hero image left (60-70%), navigation/context sidebar right. Mobile: Stacks vertically. Balances impact with clear navigation and context.

Strengths:

  • Strong visual impact + clear navigation
  • Sidebar provides context and choices
  • Asymmetric layout feels modern
  • Good use of wide desktop screens

Trade-offs:

  • Hero image smaller than full-screen options
  • Desktop/mobile experience differs significantly
  • Sidebar could compete with photography

Evaluation Criteria

As you explore the design directions, consider these factors to guide your decision:

Layout Intuitiveness

Which information hierarchy matches your priorities? Does the layout guide visitors naturally to your best work and social conversion?

Interaction Style

Which interaction style fits the "go-to portfolio" core experience? Does it support progressive discovery and calm browsing?

Visual Impact

Which direction creates the strongest first impression? Does it achieve the instant "wow" moment within 5 seconds?

Photography Dominance

Which approach best lets your photography speak for itself? Is the UI appropriately invisible?

Time-Adaptive Hero

Which direction best leverages the time-adaptive hero feature? Is this novel element showcased effectively?

Brand Alignment

Which direction best supports your calm, professional, inspired emotional goals? Does it feel distinctively "you"?

Social Conversion

Which layout best supports the 20%+ social conversion goal? Clear path to follow without being pushy?

Scalability

Which approach works across mobile and desktop? Does the experience remain strong on all devices?
